# PRIVACY POLICY
## School Early Warning Support System

**Effective Date**: February 2025  
**Last Updated**: February 2025

---

## 1. INTRODUCTION

This Privacy Policy explains how the School Early Warning Support System ("System") collects, uses, and protects student information in compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA).

---

## 2. INFORMATION WE COLLECT

### Student Information:
- Full name
- Student ID number
- Grade level
- Classroom assignment
- Attendance records
- Academic risk indicators
- Intervention case notes

### User Information:
- Username
- Email address
- Role (teacher, form master, administrator)
- Login timestamps
- IP addresses

### Automatically Collected:
- Browser type and version
- Device information
- Access times
- Pages viewed
- Actions performed

---

## 3. HOW WE USE INFORMATION

### Educational Purposes:
- Monitor student attendance
- Identify at-risk students
- Coordinate intervention strategies
- Track student progress
- Generate reports for educators

### System Operations:
- Authenticate users
- Maintain security
- Improve system performance
- Troubleshoot technical issues
- Comply with legal obligations

---

## 4. INFORMATION SHARING

### We DO Share With:
- **School Officials**: Teachers, counselors, administrators with legitimate educational interest
- **Parents/Guardians**: Upon request, regarding their own child
- **Authorized Auditors**: For compliance verification
- **Law Enforcement**: When required by court order

### We DO NOT Share With:
- Third-party marketers
- Unauthorized personnel
- Other students or parents
- Social media platforms
- Commercial entities (without consent)

---

## 5. DATA SECURITY

### Technical Measures:
- ✅ HTTPS/TLS encryption for all data transmission
- ✅ AES-256 encryption for sensitive data at rest
- ✅ Secure password hashing (bcrypt)
- ✅ Session timeout after 30 minutes of inactivity
- ✅ Rate limiting to prevent abuse

### Administrative Measures:
- Role-based access controls
- Regular security audits
- Employee training on data privacy
- Incident response procedures
- Vendor security requirements

### Physical Measures:
- Secure server facilities
- Restricted physical access
- Backup systems
- Disaster recovery plan

---

## 6. YOUR RIGHTS

### Right to Access:
Parents/guardians may request to view their child's records by contacting the school administrator.

### Right to Correct:
You may request corrections to inaccurate information through written request to the school.

### Right to Consent:
We will not disclose PII to third parties without consent, except as permitted by FERPA.

### Right to File Complaint:
You may file a complaint with:
- School Principal
- District Superintendent
- U.S. Department of Education

---

## 7. DATA RETENTION

### How Long We Keep Data:
- Active student records: Duration of enrollment
- Graduated students: 5 years after departure
- Attendance records: 3 years
- Intervention cases: 5 years after closure
- Audit logs: 7 years

### Deletion Process:
- Automated deletion based on retention schedule
- Secure overwriting of deleted data
- Verification of deletion completion
- Documentation in audit logs

---

## 8. COOKIES & TRACKING

### We Use:
- **Session Cookies**: To maintain your login session
- **Security Cookies**: To prevent unauthorized access
- **Performance Cookies**: To monitor system performance

### We Do NOT Use:
- Third-party advertising cookies
- Social media tracking pixels
- Cross-site tracking
- Behavioral profiling

---

## 9. CHILDREN'S PRIVACY

This system is designed for use by school staff to manage student information. Students do not have direct access to the system. All student data is protected under FERPA and handled in accordance with this policy.

---
