# Teacher User Manual
## AlifMonitor — School Early Warning Support System

**Version**: 1.0  
**Audience**: Teachers  
**System URL**: https://www.alifmonitor.com

---

## What This System Does for You

AlifMonitor lets you record student attendance digitally and flag students who may need support. Instead of a paper register, you mark attendance on screen. The system automatically notifies your Form Master when a student's absences reach a concerning level — you do not need to do this manually.

---

## 1. Logging In

1. Open your browser and go to **https://www.alifmonitor.com**
2. Enter your **email address** and **password** provided by your administrator
3. Click **Login**

**If login fails:**
- Check that Caps Lock is off — passwords are case-sensitive
- After many consecutive failed attempts your account is temporarily locked for 15 minutes; wait and try again

**Reminder emails:** If you have not recorded attendance by 11:00 AM on a school day, you will receive an automatic reminder email listing which classes still need attendance recorded.
- Contact your administrator if you have forgotten your password

**First login:** Your administrator sets your initial password. Change it in Settings after your first login.

---

## 2. Your Dashboard

After logging in you land on the **Teacher Dashboard**. It shows:

- Your assigned classes and subjects
- Recent alerts you have created
- Quick links to Take Attendance and Messages

The left sidebar contains all navigation. On a mobile device, tap the **☰ button** (bottom-right corner) to open the menu.

---

## 3. Recording Attendance

This is your most important daily task.

### Steps

1. Click **Take Attendance** in the sidebar
2. Select the **Classroom** — only your assigned classrooms appear
3. Select the **Subject** — only subjects you teach in that classroom appear
4. Select the **Period** (Period 1–6, Morning Session, or Afternoon Session)
5. The student list loads automatically
6. For each student, tap or click one of four buttons:
   - **Present** — student is in class
   - **Late** — student arrived after the session started
   - **Absent** — student is not present
   - **Excused** — absent with a valid reason (you must type the reason in the box that appears)
7. Click **Submit Attendance**
8. A confirmation dialog appears — click **Confirm** to save

### Important Rules

- You can only submit attendance **once per classroom, subject, and date**. If you try to submit again for the same session today, the system will tell you it is already recorded. Use **Edit Attendance** to correct mistakes.
- All students must be marked before you can submit — the Submit button stays disabled until every student has a status.
- The system pre-selects **Present** for all students when the list loads. Change only the students who are not present.

### On Mobile

On a phone, each student appears as a card with four large tap buttons. A progress bar at the top shows how many students you have marked so far.

### Keyboard Shortcuts (Desktop only)

| Shortcut | Action |
|---|---|
| Ctrl + S | Submit attendance |
| / | Jump to the student search box |
| Escape | Clear the search |

---

## 4. Editing Attendance

If you submitted attendance with a mistake:

1. Click **Edit Attendance** in the sidebar
2. Find the session by date, classroom, and subject
3. Change the status for the affected student(s)
4. Save

You can only edit sessions you created. You cannot edit another teacher's sessions.

---

## 5. Attendance Tracking

Click **Attendance Tracking** in the sidebar to view a history of all sessions you have recorded. You can filter by classroom, subject, and date range.

---

## 6. Creating a Risk Alert

If you are concerned about a student for reasons beyond attendance:

1. Go to your **Dashboard** and click the **Alerts** tab in the sidebar
2. Click **Create Alert**
3. Select the student and describe your concern
4. Submit

The alert is sent to the Form Master assigned to that student's classroom. The Form Master manages the case from there — you do not need to follow up unless they message you.

**Note:** The system also creates alerts automatically when a student has 3 or more consecutive absences. You do not need to create these manually.

---

## 7. Messages

You can send messages directly to the Form Master of your classroom:

1. Click **Messages** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Message**
3. Select the recipient (Form Master)
4. Type your message and send

Messages are private between you and the Form Master. Administrators can view message logs for compliance purposes.

---

## 8. My Classes

Click **My Classes** in the sidebar to see all classrooms and subjects assigned to you, including the number of students in each class.

---

## 9. Profile and Settings

### Changing Your Password

1. Click **Settings** in the sidebar
2. Go to the Security section
3. Enter your current password, then your new password
4. Passwords must be at least 8 characters and include: one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special character (example: `Pass123!`)

### Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds a second verification step at login using an authenticator app (e.g. Google Authenticator or Microsoft Authenticator).

- For teachers, 2FA is **optional**
- To enable: Settings → Security → Enable 2FA → scan the QR code with your authenticator app
- Once enabled, you enter a 6-digit code from the app each time you log in

---

## 10. Logging Out

Click **Logout** at the bottom of the sidebar. Your session expires automatically after 1 hour of inactivity, with a 5-minute warning before it ends.

---

## 11. Common Problems

| Problem | What to do |
|---|---|
| "Attendance already recorded" | Use Edit Attendance to correct the existing record |
| "No classes assigned" | Contact your administrator — you have no teaching assignments yet |
| Students not showing in the list | Confirm you selected the correct classroom |
| Page not loading | Check your internet connection and refresh |
| Logged out unexpectedly | Your 1-hour session expired — log in again |
| Forgot password | Contact your administrator to reset it |

---

## 12. Data Privacy

All student data in this system is confidential. You must not:
- Share your login credentials with anyone
- Screenshot or export student data for personal use
- Discuss student attendance records outside official school channels

Your actions in the system are logged for audit purposes as required by school policy.
